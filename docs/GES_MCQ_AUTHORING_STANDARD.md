# EduTrack GES MCQ Authoring Standard

This standard applies to every Basic 7, Basic 8, and Basic 9 multiple-choice question in EduTrack.

## Curriculum alignment

Each question must assess a named NaCCA curriculum indicator or an observable part of that indicator. A topic title, class level, quiz title, study habit, or definition of Bloom's taxonomy is not the learning outcome.

The authoring record for a question should identify:

- subject and Basic level;
- strand and sub-strand;
- content standard and indicator;
- cognitive process being assessed;
- expected answer and the misconception represented by each distractor.

## Level and wording

### Basic 7

- Use direct sentences and familiar school, home, market, health, farming, weather, and community contexts.
- Keep most stems below 32 words unless a short passage, table, diagram, or data set is required.
- Emphasise identification, explanation, interpretation, and one-step application.
- Introduce technical terms only when they are part of the taught indicator.

### Basic 8

- Use direct wording with enough information for two connected reasoning steps.
- Keep most stems below 40 words unless source material is required.
- Ask learners to apply concepts, compare evidence, interpret data, and explain relationships.
- Distractors should reflect errors a Basic 8 learner could reasonably make.

### Basic 9

- Use concise BECE-style scenarios, data, short passages, diagrams, and community or national issues where relevant.
- Keep most stems below 48 words unless source material is required.
- Include multi-step application, analysis of evidence, inference, and justified judgement.
- Do not make the language harder merely to make the question higher order.

Use “Basic 7”, “Basic 8”, and “Basic 9” in learner-facing curriculum language. Database values may remain `JHS1`, `JHS2`, and `JHS3` for compatibility.

## Bloom's taxonomy

- **Remember:** identify, name, state, recognise, or recall taught knowledge.
- **Understand:** explain meaning, classify, describe, summarise, or interpret.
- **Apply:** use a rule, method, concept, or procedure in a meaningful situation.
- **Analyse:** compare evidence, identify relationships, infer, distinguish causes, or detect a pattern.
- **Evaluate:** select or judge the most appropriate response using stated evidence or criteria.
- **Create:** use a constructed-response or project task. Do not label a single-answer MCQ as Create.

A scenario followed by a recall question remains recall. A long stem does not automatically represent higher-order thinking.

## Options and distractors

- Provide one clearly defensible answer and three plausible misconceptions.
- Keep options parallel in grammar, length, units, and level of detail.
- Avoid absurd distractors, clues from grammar, repeated words that reveal the answer, and overlapping answers.
- Avoid “all of the above” and “none of the above”.
- Use negative stems only when necessary and display **NOT**, **EXCEPT**, or **FALSE** clearly.
- Explanations must state why the answer is correct, not merely repeat it.

## Ghanaian context

Use Ghanaian contexts when they make the assessment more authentic: Ghana cedis, local measurements, district assemblies, sanitation, farms, markets, transport, rainfall, elections, cultural practices, and familiar names. Context must support the curriculum indicator and must not become decorative or stereotyped.

## Quiz balance

A foundation quiz should normally include approximately:

- 30% Remember/Understand;
- 40% Apply;
- 20% Analyse;
- 10% Evaluate.

An application quiz should normally include approximately:

- 10% Remember/Understand;
- 30% Apply;
- 35% Analyse;
- 25% Evaluate.

The exact balance should follow the subject indicator and assessment purpose.
