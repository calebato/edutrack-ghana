ABSTRACT
A Personalized Learning System (PLS) is an intelligent educational platform that adapts teaching
content, difficulty, and pace to suit each student’s individual learning needs, styles, and abilities,
moving away from the traditional one-size-fits-all approach to improve engagement and academic
performance.
This project designed and developed a web-based Personalized Learning System using machine
learning to address the challenges of Ghana’s junior high school education, particularly the high
rate of learning poverty affecting over 80% of students, low engagement, and significant urban-
rural and gender disparities.
The prototype was built with PHP for the backend, MySQL for the database, and HTML5 with
Bootstrap 5 for the frontend. It features custom ML predictive algorithms that analyze student
performance data to deliver personalized content recommendations, adaptive sequencing,
gamification elements (streaks, rewards, and confetti animations), a teacher analytics dashboard
with exam score prediction, and basic accessibility support.
The system was developed using the Agile Scrum methodology. The system is scheduled for pilot
testing in May–June 2026 at a public junior high school in Greater Accra.
This study offers a practical, lightweight, and locally relevant machine learning solution that
supports Ghana’s ICT in Education Policy and demonstrates the potential of personalized learning
technologies to promote equitable and inclusive education in resource-limited settings.

Education serves as the foundation for human development and societal progress, shaping how
individuals think, interact, and contribute to their communities. Education is broadly defined as
the systematic process of facilitating learning, or the acquisition of knowledge, skills, values, and

This project proposes the design and development of a personalized learning system (PLS) for

algorithms. The proposed system was built as a lightweight, web-based prototype using PHP for

1.5 Scope of the Project
This project encompasses a literature review on Machine Learning in education, requirements
gathering from Ghanaian educators and students, and the design and development of a web-based

to May 2026. This iterative approach was particularly suitable because it allowed continuous
incorporation of feedback from potential users and rapid refinement of features as the system
evolved.
The development process was divided into five main stages: requirements gathering and literature
review, system design and prototyping, implementation of core modules, integration and testing,
and preparation for pilot deployment.

experience. The Learner Profiling Module captures student interaction data such as time spent on

basic student profiles.
The Content Recommendation and Adaptive Sequencing Engine uses rule-based predictive logic

adjustable text sizes, and simplified content options to support diverse learners, including students
with disabilities.

CHAPTER TWO
LITERATURE REVIEW
2.1 Introduction
This chapter provides a comprehensive review of the literature on personalized learning systems
(PLS) using machine learning (ML) in education. It begins with a historical background of
personalized learning, tracing its evolution globally, in Europe, Africa, and specifically in Ghana.
The review then examines existing systems and technologies in depth, highlighting key platforms,
ML applications, and adaptive mechanisms. Finally, it discusses the conceptual framework for the
proposed system, building on identified gaps, and concludes with a summary of key insights.
2.2 General Background of the Study Area
Personalized learning is a pedagogical approach that tailors education to individual learners’
needs, strengths, and preferences using data-driven insights. It focuses on adaptive instruction to
improve engagement and learning outcomes, especially in diverse classrooms (Kaswan et al.,
2024).
Historically, personalized learning dates back over 200 years. Early foundations include Thomas
Jefferson’s advocacy for equitable education (1779) and systems like John Lancaster’s
Monitorial System, which grouped learners by ability and enabled scalable instruction. Later,
Benjamin Bloom introduced mastery learning in the 1960s, allowing students to progress at their
own pace. Technological advancements followed, including Sidney Pressey’s early teaching
machine and B. F. Skinner’s reinforcement-based systems. These developments led to computer-
assisted instruction and modern AI-driven adaptive learning systems, though challenges of
scalability and equity persist (Bernacki et al., 2021).
11

--- PAGE 25 ---
In Europe, personalized learning evolved through progressive, child-centered philosophies
influenced by thinkers like Jean-Jacques Rousseau. Modern implementations integrate ICT and
AI-driven platforms, particularly in countries like the UK and the Netherlands, improving
learning outcomes while addressing challenges such as teacher resistance and data privacy
concerns (Li & Wong, 2021; Walkington & Bernacki, 2020).
In Africa, adoption has been shaped by infrastructure limitations but is increasingly driven by AI.
Recent studies show that adaptive platforms improve engagement and learning outcomes in
countries like South Africa and Nigeria, while predictive analytics enhance retention rates by up
to 25% in East Africa (Bitalo, 2024; Owusu et al., 2025).
In Ghana, personalized learning aligns with national ICT policies and initiatives such as the “one
teacher, one laptop” program. AI-based systems are being explored to address foundational
literacy challenges and support inclusive education, particularly for students with disabilities.
Despite challenges like limited connectivity in rural areas, emerging AI tools show strong
potential to improve access and learning outcomes (Pan et al., 2022; Amoako et al., 2024; Opoku
et al., 2025).
2.3 Review Based on Specific Objectives
2.3.1 Learner Profiling and Gamified Engagement Module
Learner profiling is essential in personalized learning systems because it helps instruction
respond to individual student needs. A learner profile combines direct data, such as background
and learning goals, with indirect data, such as behavior, preferences, difficulties, and cognitive
ability, to support adaptive learning (Bennani et al., 2022). Machine learning improves this
process by analyzing learner interactions, quiz performance, response patterns, and progress data
12

--- PAGE 26 ---
to update learner profiles and personalize learning paths (Romero & Ventura, 2020). This allows
the system to identify different learning needs and recommend suitable support for each student.
Machine learning also strengthens learner profiling by helping systems detect patterns and
predict student performance. Predictive models can classify learners, identify students who may
be struggling, and support early intervention before performance declines (Baker, 2016). In a
practical personalized learning system, profiling can be based on simple but useful indicators
such as accuracy, time spent on tasks, quiz scores, and learning progress.
Gamification supports learner profiling by increasing motivation and engagement through
elements such as points, rewards, streaks, badges, and progress tracking. These features make
learning more interactive and encourage students to remain active in the system (Kapp, 2012).
However, gamification becomes more effective when it is adapted to different learner types and
preferences instead of applying the same rewards to every student (Marczewski, 2015).
Therefore, combining learner profiling with gamified engagement creates a more responsive
learning environment that supports motivation, participation, and improved academic
performance.
2.3.2 Machine Learning-Driven Content Recommendation and Adaptive Learning System
Machine learning-driven content recommendation systems are essential in personalized learning
because they enable the delivery of educational content tailored to individual student needs.
Akbayan Bekarystankyzy et al. (2024) explains that effective personalization combines user-
centered approaches, such as learning style models, with technology-driven adaptive systems.
Models like Kolb’s experiential learning theory and the Felder-Silverman learning style model
help classify learners based on how they process information, allowing systems to adapt content
13

--- PAGE 27 ---
to different learning preferences. At the same time, adaptive systems such as Protus demonstrate
how technology can dynamically adjust learning materials using modular and semantic
structures.
Machine learning techniques, including clustering, data mining, and recommendation algorithms,
play a critical role in analyzing learner data and delivering real-time personalized content. These
techniques enable systems to identify patterns in student performance and interaction behavior,
ensuring that learners receive content that matches their ability level and learning pace (Akbayan
Bekarystankyzy et al., 2024). This improves both engagement and academic outcomes by
maintaining an appropriate level of challenge for each learner.
However, the study also highlights key challenges, including data privacy concerns, ethical
issues, and the lack of standardized recommendation algorithms. To address these limitations,
future research is expected to focus on hybrid models that integrate machine learning with
advanced techniques such as natural language processing and dynamic adaptive algorithms.
These developments are important for improving the effectiveness and scalability of
personalized learning systems in adapting teaching to individual student needs.
2.3.3 Teacher Analytics Dashboard and Automated Performance Reporting
Teacher analytics dashboards are important components of personalized learning systems
because they provide educators with real-time insights into student performance and learning
behavior. According to Wiedbusch (2021), intelligent dashboards collect and visualize student
data such as quiz results, activity patterns, and engagement levels, enabling teachers to monitor
progress and make informed instructional decisions. These dashboards often present data through
14

--- PAGE 28 ---
visual tools like charts and activity logs, helping teachers quickly identify trends and areas where
students may need support.
Machine learning enhances these dashboards by enabling predictive analytics, which can forecast
student performance and identify at-risk learners early. Baker (2016) explains that learning
analytics systems use historical and real-time data to detect patterns and predict outcomes,
allowing teachers to intervene before students fall behind. This improves the effectiveness of
teaching by supporting timely and targeted interventions.
In addition, teacher dashboards support self-regulated learning by providing insights into student
engagement and learning processes. Wiedbusch (2021) notes that advanced dashboards can track
not only performance data but also cognitive and behavioral patterns, helping teachers
understand how students learn, not just what they achieve. However, current systems face
challenges such as limited data depth, a lack of standardization, and insufficient integration of
theoretical learning models.
Overall, integrating machine learning with teacher analytics dashboards improves decision-
making, enhances monitoring, and supports personalized instruction. In systems like your
project, dashboards that include features such as activity tracking, performance prediction, and
automated reporting can help teachers provide more effective and data-driven support to
students.

2.3.4 Scalable Real-Time Personalized Learning Framework
A scalable personalized learning framework is essential for delivering adaptive learning
experiences efficiently, especially in real-time educational environments. According to Nalam et
al. (2025), modern personalization systems use advanced machine learning architectures to
process large volumes of user data and deliver personalized recommendations instantly. These
systems rely on technologies such as distributed computing, stream processing, and caching

Software testing is a systematic process of executing a program or application with the intent of

4.2.1 Unit Testing
Unit testing is the first and lowest level of software testing. It involves verifying the correctness of
individual units or components of the code (such as functions, methods, or classes) in isolation
36

--- PAGE 50 ---
from the rest of the system (GeeksforGeeks, 2026). The primary goal is to identify and fix defects
early in the development process, which reduces the cost and effort required for later debugging.
In the proposed system, unit testing was applied extensively to core modules. For example:

learner profiling) were tested to prevent SQL injection and verify data integrity.
These tests were performed manually with structured test cases during each sprint, aligning with
Agile best practices where developers write and run unit tests before integrating code (Atlassian,
2021). Early detection of issues in these isolated units ensured a stable foundation for higher-level
testing.

4.5 Implementation Challenges
4.5.1 ML Implementation
Predictive algorithms were coded directly in PHP using a combination of rule-based logic and
statistical methods. These algorithms process student interaction logs stored in the MySQL
database to generate predictions and recommendations. For instance:
• Exam score prediction on the teacher dashboard uses weighted averages and threshold-
based rules derived from a student’s recent quiz performance, completion times, and
accuracy trends.
• At-risk student identification applies simple decision rules (e.g., if accuracy falls below
60% over multiple sessions or streaks are broken repeatedly).
• Adaptive content sequencing employs if-then rules that adjust topic difficulty and
recommend next learning materials based on the student’s current knowledge level and
learning pace.
• Learner profiling aggregates interaction data (hints requested, time spent per question,
and subject strengths) to classify basic learning patterns without complex neural networks.
This rule-based predictive approach provided transparency, fast execution, and ease of debugging
while effectively demonstrating the intended personalization features. Rule-based systems are
widely recognized as a practical alternative in early-stage educational prototypes, especially in
low-resource environments, because they offer interpretability and low overhead compared to deep
learning models (Peng et al., 2019; Bellarhmouch et al., 2022; Wearebrain, 2025). The coded
predictive algorithms were integrated seamlessly into the modular PHP architecture, allowing real-
time updates as new student data is logged during the pilot phase.
59

--- PAGE 73 ---
The database schema was intentionally designed with detailed logging tables (quiz_attempts,
interaction_logs, student_progress) to support these predictive algorithms and to facilitate future
enhancement with more advanced machine learning models when additional resources become
available.
4.5.2 Limited Access to Diverse Real-World Datasets
Effective predictive algorithms require sufficient and varied student performance data. During
development, real Ghanaian JHS interaction data were unavailable, so synthetic and demo data
were seeded into the edutrack.sql database file. The system was built with robust data collection
mechanisms to capture authentic usage data during the planned pilot study (starting from week
three of the May–June 2026 pilot). This will allow refinement and calibration of the predictive
rules based on real student behavior.
4.5.3 Infrastructure and Connectivity Constraints
Public junior high schools in Ghana often operate with limited internet bandwidth, intermittent
power, and low-end devices. Heavy external machine learning libraries would have been
impractical. By coding the predictive algorithms directly in lightweight PHP and MySQL, the
system achieves response times under 2 seconds for recommendations and analytics, even on
modest hardware. The Bootstrap 5 frontend further ensures responsiveness across desktops,
tablets, and mobile phones commonly available in schools.

project and the Ghanaian educational context.
The predictive algorithms were implemented using rule-based logic and statistical methods coded
directly in PHP, rather than full-scale machine learning models. While effective for demonstration
and pilot purposes, this hybrid approach lacks the sophistication and accuracy of advanced neural
networks or reinforcement learning for deeper personalization (Peng et al., 2019).
Also, the development relied on synthetic and demo data due to limited access to large-scale real
JHS student interaction datasets during the project timeline. Real pilot data collection will begin
only in the May–June 2026 testing phase, meaning the predictive rules have not yet been fully
calibrated or validated with authentic Ghanaian learner data.
Finally, ethical considerations around data privacy were addressed through security measures
(PDO prepared statements, bcrypt hashing, CSRF protection), but comprehensive institutional
review beyond university ethics approval and planned pilot consent processes was beyond the
project’s immediate scope.
These limitations do not undermine the prototype’s value as a foundational step but highlight areas
for future enhancement.
5.4 Recommendations for Future Research
Based on the findings and limitations identified in this study, several important directions are
proposed for future research and development to strengthen and extend the personalized learning
system.
Future studies should expand the pilot implementation into larger-scale, multi-school trials
conducted across both urban and rural settings in different regions of Ghana. These expanded pilots
64

--- PAGE 78 ---
should incorporate longitudinal assessments spanning at least one full academic year. Such
extended evaluations would make it possible to measure the long-term impact of the system on
key educational outcomes, including improvements in student engagement, academic performance
in national examinations, reduction in dropout rates, and greater equity for students with
disabilities. This approach would generate stronger evidence regarding the effectiveness and
scalability of personalized learning technologies within the diverse Ghanaian educational
landscape.
In addition, future work should focus on enhancing the multimodal and inclusive capabilities of
the platform. Researchers should develop advanced accessibility tools, including fine-tuned
speech-to-text and text-to-speech systems that accurately support Ghanaian English accents as well
as major local languages. Incorporating emotion-aware adaptation mechanisms and a wider range
of learning modalities, such as video, audio content, and interactive simulations, would make the
system more responsive to different learning styles. These improvements would be especially
valuable for students with disabilities and those in low literacy environments, thereby increasing
the overall inclusivity and pedagogical effectiveness of the platform.
Furthermore, addressing infrastructure and scalability challenges should remain a priority in future
research. Studies should explore the development of offline-first capabilities, edge computing
solutions, and low-cost hardware optimizations to ensure reliable performance even in schools
facing unstable internet and electricity supply. Researchers should also investigate cloud hybrid
deployment models that fully comply with the Ghana Data Protection Act while remaining
affordable and sustainable for public junior high schools. Such efforts would help bridge the digital
divide and support wider national adoption of personalized learning systems across Ghana.

5.5 Conclusion
This project successfully designed and developed EduTrack Ghana, a practical personalized
learning system that demonstrates how predictive algorithms and gamification can adapt teaching
to individual JHS students' needs in Ghana. By addressing the limitations of traditional one-size-
fits-all methods through an engaging, teacher-supportive, and deployable web prototype, the study
contributes to the growing body of work on AI-inspired educational technologies in resource-
constrained African settings.
The system provides a scalable starting point that enhances student motivation via streaks, badges,
and immediate feedback; equips teachers with actionable analytics and automated reports; and lays
the groundwork for data-driven personalization. While limitations related to data, infrastructure,
and scope exist, the Agile development process and hybrid predictive approach ensured a
functional, secure, and ready-for-pilot solution.
Ultimately, the system has the transformative potential of personalized learning systems to
promote equity, improve engagement, and support foundational literacy and numeracy in Ghana.
With continued refinement, larger pilots, and policy support, such initiatives can play a vital role
in building a more inclusive and effective education system. The project reaffirms that technology,
when thoughtfully adapted to local contexts, can be a powerful tool for human development and
societal progress.
